    Saftey for Drivers

    Seatbelt Use: Always wear your seatbelt and ensure all passengers do the same. Adjust your seat and steering wheel to a comfortable and safe position. Avoid Distractions: Keep your focus on the road. Avoid using your phone, eating, or engaging in other distracting activities while driving. Set up navigation and music before you start driving. Obey Traffic Laws: Follow posted speed limits and traffic signals. Use turn signals and follow proper lane discipline. Maintain a Safe Following Distance: Keep a safe distance from the vehicle in front of you to allow for reaction time in case of sudden stops. Regular Vehicle Maintenance: Ensure your vehicle is in good condition by performing regular maintenance checks on brakes, tires, lights, and fluids. Stay Sober: Never drive under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or any substances that can impair your ability to drive. Be Aware of Weather Conditions: Adjust your driving to suit the weather conditions. Slow down and increase your following distance in adverse weather. Use Your Mirrors: Check your mirrors regularly to be aware of your surroundings. Be Patient and Courteous: Stay calm in traffic and be courteous to other drivers.

    Saftey for Riders

    Buckle Up: Always wear your seatbelt, regardless of where you are seated in the vehicle. Avoid Distracting the Driver: Minimize distractions for the driver. Let them focus on the road. Obey Traffic Rules: Follow the driver's instructions and adhere to traffic rules. Keep Hands and Feet Inside the Vehicle: Avoid hanging limbs outside the vehicle, especially when it's in motion. Use Child Safety Seats: Ensure that children are in appropriate car seats based on their age, weight, and height. Inform the Driver of Any Concerns: If you notice something wrong or feel uncomfortable with the driver's behavior, communicate it politely. Be Mindful of the Driver's Concentration: Refrain from engaging in activities that may distract the driver. Exit Safely: Wait for the vehicle to come to a complete stop before getting in or out. Use designated entrances and exits. Remember, safety is a shared responsibility. Both drivers and passengers play a crucial role in ensuring a safe and secure travel experience.
